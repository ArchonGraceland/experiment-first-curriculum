# Experiment Upgrade Notes — $5,000 Budget

This document describes how each module's experiment improves with proper lab equipment. The original experiment guide (experiment_guide.md) describes the full procedure — this document covers only what changes with the upgraded equipment.

**Budget spent:** $3,228 | **Remaining:** $1,772 (reserve for replacements, additional consumables, or supplementary materials)

---

## How the Equipment Changes the Experience

### The Big Upgrades (equipment that transforms multiple modules)

**Photogate Timer System ($95)** — Used in P1, P2, P3, P11, P13
- Replaces phone stopwatch with millisecond-precision timing
- Student measures velocity and acceleration to 3 significant figures
- P2 goes from "it took about 1.2 seconds" to "terminal velocity = 1.847 m/s ± 0.003"
- Research reports suddenly have real precision data to analyze

**Dynamics Track with Carts ($90)** — Used in P1, P3, P11
- Replaces ruler groove and marbles with a proper low-friction track
- Carts have built-in spring launchers and Velcro for elastic/inelastic collisions
- P11 (momentum) goes from "the marble seemed to transfer its speed" to measured before/after velocities with conservation verified to within 5%

**Compound Microscope ($250)** — Used in B1, B2, B5, B8
- Replaces phone clip with 40x-1000x magnification
- B1: see individual organelles, not just blobs
- B5: clearly see chromosomes in mitosis stages
- Oil immersion at 1000x makes bacteria visible in B2
- This single purchase transforms all biology observation modules

**CO₂ Sensor + Dissolved Oxygen Probe ($200 combined)** — Used in B3, B10, E5, AE1
- Replaces qualitative indicators with quantitative measurements
- B3 goes from counting bubbles to plotting O₂ concentration vs. light intensity curves
- E5 goes from "this jar feels warmer" to "CO₂ at 800ppm produced a 3.2°C increase vs. 0.4°C in the control"
- B10 carbon cycle becomes measurable: actual ppm readings in sealed containers with and without plants

**Digital pH Meter ($45)** — Used in C1, C9, C12, AC3, AE1
- Replaces cabbage juice / pH strips with 0.01 resolution
- C1: student measures pH of every substance to two decimal places
- AC3 titration: student can plot a real titration curve with pH readings every 0.5mL of titrant
- AE1 ocean acidification: measure the actual pH drop as CO₂ is added

**Precision Scale ($60)** — Used in C3, C10, C11, C14
- Replaces kitchen scale with 0.01g resolution
- C11 conservation of mass: measure before and after to hundredths of a gram — conservation is demonstrated to 0.01g accuracy
- C14 calorimetry: weigh the food before and after burning to calculate exact mass consumed

**Telescope ($250)** — Used in E7, E8, AE4
- No budget equivalent exists
- E7: student sees Saturn's rings, Jupiter's moons, craters on our Moon
- E8: observe star colors directly, see the Orion Nebula, double stars
- AE4: see galaxies as fuzzy patches of light — the same objects whose redshift proves expansion

**Van de Graaff Generator ($120)** — Used in P5
- Replaces balloon experiments with dramatic, visceral demonstrations
- Hair standing on end, sparks jumping to a grounding sphere, feeling electrostatic repulsion
- The student can measure spark gap distance to estimate voltage (30kV/cm in air)
- Makes electrostatic force tangible in a way a balloon can't

## What the $1,772 Reserve Can Cover

With $1,772 remaining in the budget, here are recommended uses:

**Replacements and breakage** (~$200): Glass breaks, probes fail, consumables run out. Budget a repair/replacement fund.

**Additional consumables** (~$300): A second year of chemistry reagents, agar plates, seeds, batteries, and other single-use materials.

**Field trip or guest speaker** (~$200): A visit to a local university lab, museum, or observatory gives context to the home experiments.

**Student choice equipment** (~$500): Let the student pick equipment for their Phase 4 "Designer" modules. If they design their own experiment, they should be able to request materials. This is a powerful motivator.

**Expansion into Earth & Space** (~$300): Additional geological specimens, a rock tumbler ($40–60), a portable weather station for outdoor fieldwork, binoculars for astronomy.

**Digital tools** (~$200): A year of a data logging app subscription, 3D molecular modeling software, or a physics simulation suite (PhET is free, but others like Labster or Vernier Video Analysis add value).
